Why Acne-Prone Skin Needs Sunscreen Every Day

Prevents Darker Acne Marks

Acne often leaves behind post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH). Sun exposure stimulates melanin, causing these marks to become darker and last longer.

Protects Skin During Treatment

Treatments like Retinoids, Salicylic Acid, or Adapalene increase sun sensitivity. Sunscreen prevents secondary irritation and barrier damage.

Reduces Inflammation & Redness

Modern formulas with Niacinamide or Zinc Oxide help calm inflamed skin and support active barrier repair.

Prevents Premature Aging

UV exposure breaks down collagen, accelerating fine lines and enlarging pores—often a side effect of chronic acne.

How to Identify an Acne-Safe Sunscreen

1

Look for "Non-Comedogenic"

This is your primary filter. It means the formula is specifically designed not to clog pores. Look for "Oil-free" and "Dermatologist-tested".

2

Choose SPF 50 or Higher

For intense Indian UV indices, SPF 30 is the bare minimum, but SPF 50+ is ideal for outdoor exposure and pigmentation prevention.

3

Understand PA Ratings (PA++++)

PA ratings indicate UVA protection. For Indian skin prone to tanning and marks, PA++++ is the recommended standard.

4

Demand Modern UV Filters

Prefer photostable filters like Tinosorb S, Tinosorb M, or Uvinul A Plus. They last longer and are less irritating than older chemicals like Oxybenzone.

5

Select the Right Texture

Opt for Aqua Gels, Water Fluids, or Silicone Gels. Avoid heavy, greasy creams if you have extremely oily skin.
